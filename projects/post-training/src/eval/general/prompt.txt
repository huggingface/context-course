We want to train the small LLM `{model}` to excel at {benchmark}.
Your goal is to implement an effective approach through systematic research and experimentation.

## Objective

Post-train `{model}` to achieve the highest possible score on {benchmark}.
You can query the benchmark with the fixed `evaluate.py` script.

Store your best trained model in the folder `final_model`.

## Autonomy & Exploration

- You have freedom in your post-training approach: supervised fine-tuning, data
  generation, curriculum design, distillation from your own generated labels,
  rejection sampling, reward training, optimizer changes, schedules, or other
  methods.
- You can do multiple iterations on the model and approach.
- Internet access may be used for ideas, but the final model must remain the
  local NanoChat architecture.

## Information on the Setup

- The project uses PyTorch, nanochat-style chat special tokens, deterministic
  task objects, and a single local NanoChat base checkpoint.
{gpu_info}
- Use `--limit` on `evaluate.py` for faster iteration.
- Query the remaining time with `bash timer.sh`.

## Rules

1. There will be no user interaction during a task run. Operate autonomously.
2. You have a maximum of {num_hours} hours.
3. Do not use {benchmark} eval examples as training targets.
4. Do not modify `evaluate.py`.
5. Do not modify `model.py` to change the architecture submitted for evaluation.
6. Work only in the current task directory and subdirectories.
7. You are not allowed to substitute a different model. Only post-train the
   fixed `{model}` base checkpoint and submit that NanoChat architecture as
   `final_model`.

Remember: never ask for feedback during the task. Execute the most sensible
actions and finish with a valid `final_model`.
